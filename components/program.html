Our focus areas for Rural Development

Our Rural development activities, key areas are Education & Health, Sustainable Livelihood, and Social causes.

For the past (3) years we have been working in

Education
Social development
Other (Women and Child Rights)

The presence of street children worker, in both, doing hard work or street work such as polishing shoes, cleaning car windows, collecting garbage, and other works have become a normal phenomenon. Labor Children not only violates the natural foundations and human rights but also poses a serious threat to the social future, economic growth, and development of society. To support working girls, REHSO first collects them from the streets and then registers them through administrative procedures. Creating a safe educational place, providing health services, commuting expenses, breakfast, lunch, artistic activities training, vocational training, providing psychological counseling, providing clothes, necessary health supplies, stationery, and holding recreational and scientific tours are the services that are intended for them and will continue until the end of high school.

In Afghanistan, children face various problems due to poverty and economic difficulties, which are the root of all abnormalities in poverty. Social problems, such as forcing children to laborious works, will not go away until poverty is eradicated. Due to their problems, these children have to earn money along with their parents and support their families. REHSO has planned a program for these hard-working children, who are the future creators of society, and poor families to provide monthly stationery for 300 boys and girls so they can continue their education.

Poor families face many problems daily. On the other hand, due to the staggering costs of treatment in the country, health problems have become a nightmare for them. These vulnerable families are not able to afford their medical expenses and are deprived of proper health services. Therefore, REHSO has decided to include a program to support the needy patients, considering the needs of the people in its community. The foundation works in the area of medical services to support working children and poor families. It should be noted that REHSO, for support of the needy patients, has promised to cooperate with 150 patients with hemophilia and thalassemia in the preparation of their drugs on a monthly basis in an agreement with the Director of Herat Regional Hospital.

In this section, we try to provide employment opportunities for children under our auspices by vo- cational and technical education and help them to become self-sufficient. We have volunteers in the areas of sewing, painting, calligraphy, and handicrafts training by our side.

Street children's workers are the most vulnerable group in society. Their bitter past has caused them to suffer a lot of mental and psychological damage, and if these injuries are not diagnosed and treated, there is the possibility of more dangerous mental illnesses. Due to the community needs and lack of child psychologists, REHSO will launch specialized child training courses for 20 graduated psychologists from (Clinical and Educational psychology) by experienced international professors, and after completing the training courses, these psychologists, along with the REHSO, will provide free individual and group counseling services under the supervision of volunteer specialists for community needy children.
